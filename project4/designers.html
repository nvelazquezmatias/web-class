Designers

Michel Boyer

Michel Boyer was a French interior and product designer known for his modern and minimalist designs. He was a part of the mid-20th-century modernist design movement, inspired by simple lines, bold shapes, and a harmonious balance of form and function.

Boyer honed his skills at the prestigious Ecole Nationale Superieure des Beaux-Arts in Paris, where he pursued architectural studies. He began his career as an architect, then later transitioned into furniture design, designing tables, chairs, and cabinets.

Pierre Jeanneret

Pierre Jeanneret was a Swiss architect, furniture designer, and Le Corbusier's cousin. They collaborated on various architectural and furniture projects. He significantly influenced modern furniture design for many of Le Corbusier's buildings.

Charlotte Perriand

Charlotte Perriand, a French architect and interior designer, was renowned for her collaborations with Corbusier and Jeanneret, which helped shape modern furniture today. Her designs used natural materials and the principle of functionality. Her famous works are the LC4 chaise longue and the Grand Confort series.

Piero Lissoni

Piero Lissoni is an internationally renowned Italian architect and designer known for his modern and minimalistic style. Hailing from Seregno, Italy, he earned a degree in architecture from the prestigious Polytechnic University of Milan.

Lissoni approaches design from simplicity, clarity, and user-centered principles. His designs are timeless, functional, and visually appealing.

In 1986, he founded a studio, Lissoni & Partners, in Milan, recognized for its architecture, interior design, and industrial design. Over the years, Lissoni has collaborated with prestigious design brands such as Kartell, B&B Italia, Cassina, Fritz Hansen, and Alessi.

Lissoni has collaborated with renowned furniture manufacturers, creating elegant and modern furniture pieces. His designs often feature a timeless quality, making them suitable for various interior styles.
