THE GREAT GATSBY

F. Scott Fitzgerald

1925

The Great Gatsby is a novel by F. Scott Fitzgerald written in 1925. The book follows the love story of Jay Gatsby and Daisy Buchanan. It delves into the luxury lifestyle of the Roaring Twenties and the clash between new wealth and old money set in 2022.

Main Character

Jay Gatsby

Jay Gatsby is wealthy new-money man who throws lavish parties in hopes of reuniting with his former lover.

Nick Carraway, a man from the Midwest, narrates the story. He moves to Long Island, where he meets and befriends Gatsby. Daisy Buchanan, Nick's cousin, is married to Tom Buchanan and is also Gatsby's former lover. Tom Buchanan is a wealthy man who cheats on Daisy with a woman named Myrtle Wilson. Myrtle Wilson, married to George Wilson, is Tom's mistress. George Wilson is a mechanic in the Valley of Ashes, a place full of industrial waste.

Nick becomes the key connection between Gatsby and Daisy.

Tom starts to suspect Gatsby's newfound wealth and intentions towards Daisy. Although Daisy was once in love with Gatsby, she decided to marry Tom due to his wealth. In the hopes of winning Daisy back, Gatsby accumulated wealth.

Daisy and Gatsby make plans to run away, but George, the husband of Myrtle, interrupts and shoots Gatsby as he waits for Daisy's call. In a sacrificial act of love, Gatsby takes responsibility for Daisy's hit-and-run accident, which caused Myrtle's death, hoping it would win Daisy's heart. Unfortunately, Daisy chooses her wealth and status over Gatsby, leading to his premature death. Nick calls Gatsby, but George shoots him before Gatsby can answer, and he dies, believing Daisy was reaching out to him.
